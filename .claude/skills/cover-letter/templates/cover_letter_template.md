# Cover Letter Template

This template follows the structure established in Gavin's successful cover letters for Anthropic and M&G.

## Standard Structure

### Header
```
Gavin Neil Slater
7 Fir Tree Close
Esher
KT10 9DS
[Date]
```

### Salutation
- If known contact: "Dear [Name],"
- If unknown: "Dear [Company] Hiring Team," or "Dear Hiring Manager,"

### Opening Paragraph (The Hook)
Options:
1. **Event/Presentation Reference**: "I thoroughly enjoyed your [keynote/presentation] at [event]..."
2. **Company Strategy Reference**: "I am excited by [Company]'s strategic push into [area]..."
3. **Role-Specific Opening**: "I am writing to express my interest in the [Role] position..."
4. **Mutual Connection**: "Following my conversation with [Name], I am writing..."

### "Why Am I a Good Fit" Section
Introduce 3-4 key areas that match the role. Format as bold bullets or numbered list:

**Example structure:**
"Why am I a good fit for this role? These are the [number] key areas I bring to the table:
- [Key area 1 - most relevant to role]
- [Key area 2 - complementary strength]
- [Key area 3 - unique differentiator]
- [Key area 4 - bonus strength if applicable]"

### Evidence Sections (2-4 sections)
Each section should have:
- **Bold header** describing the competency
- Concrete examples with metrics where possible
- Specific company/project names
- Relevance to the target role

**Common sections:**
1. **Practical Hands-on AI Experience**
   - Personal AI Infrastructure (15 agents)
   - Risk Agents platform development
   - Credit Risk Workflow app (60% efficiency improvement)

2. **Deep Financial Services Knowledge**
   - 30+ years experience
   - Tier-1 institutions: Barclays Capital, Deutsche Bank, ICBC, Nordea
   - Regulatory expertise: FSA, Fed, SEC, Basel implementations

3. **Proven Track Record of Change Management**
   - Lehman Brothers integration (10,000 employees, 3 months, during 2008 crisis)
   - Nordea transformation (EUR125M programme)
   - ICBC greenfield data platform

4. **Understanding Both Sides of the Table**
   - Seller perspective: Stream Financial co-founder
   - Buyer perspective: Enterprise technology procurement at major banks
   - Stakeholder alignment across risk, compliance, technology, business

### Industry-Specific Insights (Optional)
If applicable, show understanding of specific challenges:
- AI adoption challenges in financial services
- Regulatory requirements for AI explainability
- Cultural change management for AI transformation

### Why This Company (Optional but Powerful)
Show genuine interest in the specific company:
- Reference their products, strategy, or values
- Explain unique fit between your experience and their needs
- Show you've done research

### Closing Paragraph
Standard elements:
- Express enthusiasm for opportunity
- Reference enclosed CV
- Offer to discuss specific areas
- Clear call to action

**Example:**
"I would welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience can contribute to [Company]'s [mission/goals] in the [area] sector. Thank you for your consideration."

### Sign-off
```
Sincerely,
Gavin Slater
```

### P.S. (Optional - for emphasis)
Use to:
- Emphasize a key point
- Add timely information
- Create memorable final impression

## Tailoring Guidelines by Role Type

### AI/Technology Companies (e.g., Anthropic)
- Lead with AI implementation experience
- Emphasize first-principles learning
- Reference specific Claude/AI experience
- Highlight technical projects
- Show understanding of AI safety/ethics

### Financial Services (e.g., M&G, Banks)
- Lead with financial services credibility
- Emphasize regulatory expertise
- Focus on transformation track record
- Highlight risk management background
- Show understanding of compliance requirements

### Consulting/Advisory
- Lead with breadth of experience
- Emphasize business building (Stream Financial)
- Focus on client relationship management
- Highlight strategic thinking capability
- Show sector expertise

### Enterprise Sales
- Lead with understanding buyer perspective
- Emphasize relationship building
- Focus on complex deal navigation
- Highlight stakeholder management
- Show domain credibility

## Avoid
- Generic statements without evidence
- Repeating CV content verbatim
- Over-long paragraphs
- Passive voice where active is stronger
- Clichés ("passionate about...", "team player...")
- Salary discussion (unless specifically requested)
- Negative comments about current/past employers
